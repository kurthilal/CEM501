# CEM501 Reading List

**Course:** CEM501 Communication Skills for CEM | **Spring 2026** | **Bogazici University**
**Instructor:** Dr. Eyuphan Koc

---

## Professional Communication in Engineering

1. **Beer, D. & McMurrey, D.** (2019). *A Guide to Writing as an Engineer* (5th ed.). Wiley.
   Comprehensive reference for technical writing in engineering contexts -- memos, reports, proposals, and correspondence.

2. **Lannon, J. M. & Gurak, L. J.** (2020). *Technical Communication* (15th ed.). Pearson.
   Broad coverage of communication strategies for technical professionals, including audience analysis, document design, and oral presentations.

3. **Pfeiffer, W. S. & Adkins, K. E.** (2012). *Technical Communication Fundamentals*. Pearson.
   Concise treatment of workplace communication with emphasis on clarity, conciseness, and professional tone.

---

## Construction Management Communication

4. **Gould, F. E. & Joyce, N. E.** (2014). *Construction Project Management* (4th ed.). Pearson.
   Chapters 8-10 cover project correspondence, meeting management, RFIs, submittals, and change order documentation.

5. **Hinze, J.** (2012). *Construction Planning and Scheduling* (4th ed.). Pearson.
   Relevant sections on schedule communication, progress reporting, and stakeholder coordination in construction projects.

6. **Levy, S. M.** (2018). *Project Management in Construction* (7th ed.). McGraw-Hill.
   Practical guide with extensive treatment of owner-architect-contractor communication protocols and dispute resolution procedures.

---

## AI & LLMs for Professionals

7. **Anthropic.** (2025). "Prompt Engineering Guide."
   https://docs.anthropic.com/en/docs/build-with-claude/prompt-engineering/overview
   Official guide to effective prompting for Claude models -- essential reading for the email agent project.

8. **White, J. et al.** (2023). "A Prompt Pattern Catalog to Enhance Prompt Engineering with ChatGPT." *arXiv preprint arXiv:2302.11382*.
   Systematic catalog of reusable prompt patterns; useful framework for designing classification and drafting prompts.

9. **Mollick, E.** (2024). *Co-Intelligence: Living and Working with AI*. Portfolio/Penguin.
   Accessible overview of how professionals across fields are integrating LLMs into their workflows, with practical advice on human-AI collaboration.

---

## Agentic AI & Automation

10. **Anthropic.** (2025). "Building Effective Agents."
    https://www.anthropic.com/research/building-effective-agents
    Key reference for the course project -- design patterns for LLM-based agents including tool use, orchestration, and evaluation.

11. **Weng, L.** (2023). "LLM Powered Autonomous Agents." *Lil'Log*.
    https://lilianweng.github.io/posts/2023-06-23-agent/
    Thorough survey of agentic architectures: planning, memory, tool use, and multi-agent systems.

12. **Shinn, N. et al.** (2023). "Reflexion: Language Agents with Verbal Reinforcement Learning." *NeurIPS 2023*.
    Introduces the concept of agents that reflect on and learn from their own outputs -- relevant to iterative draft improvement.

---

## Negotiation & Conflict Resolution

13. **Fisher, R., Ury, W. & Patton, B.** (2011). *Getting to Yes: Negotiating Agreement Without Giving In* (3rd ed.). Penguin.
    The foundational text on interest-based (principled) negotiation. Required reading before Week 10 role-plays.

14. **Cheung, S. O. & Yiu, T. W.** (2006). "Are Construction Disputes Inevitable?" *IEEE Transactions on Engineering Management*, 53(3), 456-470.
    Empirical study of dispute causes in construction projects, with a communication-centered framework for dispute avoidance.

---

## Cross-Cultural Communication

15. **Meyer, E.** (2014). *The Culture Map: Breaking Through the Invisible Boundaries of Global Business*. PublicAffairs.
    Practical framework for understanding communication differences across cultures -- especially relevant for international construction projects.

16. **Loosemore, M. & Muslmani, H. S. A.** (1999). "Construction Project Management in the Persian Gulf: Inter-Cultural Communication." *International Journal of Project Management*, 17(2), 95-100.
    Case study of cross-cultural communication challenges on large-scale construction projects, with lessons applicable to Turkey's international contracting sector.
